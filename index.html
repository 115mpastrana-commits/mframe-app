M-FRAME gives every athlete — beginner or elite, any sport, any age — the same weekly target: 27 M. Hit it consistently and fitness comes. The number measures workout quality relative to your own threshold, not absolute performance.
Read the full explanation▾
What is M?

One number = Load Points + Intensity Points. Each ranges from 1 to 3, so one session scores between 2 and 6 M, and a full week maxes out at 42 M.

The sport doesn't matter — a hard 60-min swim and a hard 60-min run are both M4. Only effort and duration count.

Rest days are 0 M. The week resets every Monday.

27
The universal fitness target

It doesn't matter if you're 22 or 65. If you run, swim, bike, or do all three. If you have 15 minutes or 3 hours a day. If you're a beginner or a competitive athlete.

Hit 27 M consistently for 8–12 weeks and you will measurably improve — regardless of sport, age, or starting point.

Not because of the number itself — but because 27 M encodes the right amount of metabolic stress, at the right intensity, with the right recovery built in.

M-Score Zones
Healthy Habits (2–9 M)
Very slow fitness loss, preserving routine. Acceptable for 1–2 weeks during life stress.
Slow Detraining (10–13 M)
Gradual fitness loss. Correct within 2–3 weeks or reduce TSS goal.
Maintenance (14–18 M)
Fitness preserved, fatigue cleared. Target for recovery weeks. Sustainable indefinitely.
Productive (19–24 M)
Fitness building. Target for most training weeks. Sustainable 8–12 weeks.
Optimal (25–27 M)
Peak adaptation. Sustainable 3–4 weeks. Must follow with Maintenance.
Overreaching (28–29 M)
Functional overload. Maximum 1 week. Follow with Maintenance.
Injury Risk (30+ M)
Maladaptation. Reduce load immediately.
